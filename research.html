Programs and studies

Research

Our projects examine how mind-body practices and complementary therapies influence physiology, symptom burden, recovery, and health-related quality of life.

Mind-body intervention studies

We investigate the effects of Qigong and Tai Chi on stress biology, autonomic regulation, physical function, and patient-reported wellbeing.

Comparative therapy research

Lab studies compare mind-body approaches with physical exercise, heat treatment, massage therapy, acupuncture, and herbal applications.

Technology-enabled measurement

We use physiological recording, biomedical instrumentation, and data-rich assessment methods to quantify intervention-associated change.

Translational health applications

Findings are intended to support stronger integration of evidence-based complementary therapies into whole-person health and clinical care.
