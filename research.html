Translational Science

Research

Investigating the physiological and bioenergetic mechanisms of complementary and alternative therapies through rigorous biomedical measurement.

Qigong & Tai Chi

Our primary focus is on the measurable effects of Qigong and Tai Chi practice. Using real-time physiological monitoring, we track changes in heart rate variability, respiration, skin conductance, and bioelectrical markers before, during, and after practice sessions.

Comparative Therapy Studies

We compare the effects of mind-body practices against physical exercise, heat treatment, massage therapy, acupuncture, and topical herbal medicine. This comparative approach helps isolate the unique contributions of each modality.

Bioenergy Measurement

The lab employs biomedical technologies to study changes in bioenergy markers associated with complementary therapies. This includes measuring biophoton emission, bioelectrical impedance, and other physiological signals.

Integrative Medicine

Our research aims to build a scientific foundation for whole-person health and healing. By generating rigorous evidence, we hope to accelerate the integration of evidence-based complementary approaches with Western medicine.
