Lab Director

Shin Lin, Ph.D.

Professor of Developmental & Cell Biology, Biomedical Engineering, and Integrative Medicine

Biography

Dr. Shin Lin is the director of the Laboratory for Mind-Body Signaling and Energy Research at the University of California, Irvine. He holds appointments in the Department of Developmental & Cell Biology, the Department of Biomedical Engineering, and the Susan Samueli Center for Integrative Medicine.

His research lies at the intersection of molecular cell biology, biophysics, and integrative medicine. Dr. Lin has spent decades investigating the physiological and bioenergetic mechanisms underlying traditional mind-body practices such as Qigong and Tai Chi, with the goal of accelerating the integration of evidence-based complementary therapies into Western medical practice.

A central theme of his work is the use of advanced biomedical technologies — including real-time physiological monitoring, bioelectrical measurement, and molecular analysis — to quantify changes associated with complementary and alternative medicine (CAM) interventions.

Research Focus

  • Physiological effects of Qigong and Tai Chi
  • Comparative studies with exercise, massage, acupuncture, and herbal medicine
  • Bioenergy markers and bioelectrical measurement
  • Real-time physiological monitoring during mind-body practices
  • Integration of complementary therapies with Western medicine

Lab Research Themes

The lab employs an extensive collection of biomedical technologies to study changes in physiological and bioenergy markers associated with various complementary and alternative therapies. Current work focuses on the effects of Qigong and Tai Chi, comparing these to physical exercise, heat treatment, massage therapy, acupuncture, and topical herbal medicine.

By applying rigorous measurement approaches drawn from cell biology, biophysics, and biomedical engineering, the lab aims to build a scientific foundation for whole-person health and healing.
