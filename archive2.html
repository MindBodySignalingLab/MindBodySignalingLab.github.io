About the lab

The Laboratory for Mind-Body Signaling and Energy Research is affiliated with the Department of Developmental & Cell Biology, the Department of Biomedical Engineering, and the Susan Samueli Center for Integrative Medicine at the University of California, Irvine.

Our research involves an extensive collection of biomedical technologies to study changes in physiological and bioenergy markers associated with complementary and alternative therapies — always with a holistic yin-yang perspective.

Current focus

We study the effects of Qigong and Tai Chi and compare those effects with physical exercise, heat treatment, massage therapy, acupuncture, and topical herbal medicine.

The goal is to accelerate the integration of evidence-based complementary approaches with Western medicine to enhance healing, resilience, and overall wellbeing — balancing the dynamic forces of yin and yang.

What we do

We combine mind-body practice, physiology, engineering, and clinical collaboration to better understand how subtle interventions — like Tai Chi and Qigong — influence health outcomes.

Who we serve

Our work supports researchers, clinicians, students, and community participants interested in integrative medicine and measurable pathways of healing.
